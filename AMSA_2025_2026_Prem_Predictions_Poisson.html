Poisson Distribution Model — used by professional betting companies and sports analysts.

Each team's attack strength and defense strength is calibrated from all goals scored and conceded. For each remaining match, the model computes the expected goals (λ) for each team, then uses the Poisson formula to build a full probability matrix of every possible scoreline (0–0 through 8–8). Win/draw/loss probabilities are derived from this matrix.

10,000 simulations are run and aggregated. Results show title %, relegation % (9th/10th finish), points range, and most likely final position — all stable across page loads.

How Poisson strength ratings work:

Attack strength = team's average goals scored ÷ league average goals scored per game
Defense strength = team's average goals conceded ÷ league average goals conceded per game

A value > 1.0 means above average. Expected goals for a match: λ_home = attack_home × defense_away × avg_home_goals and λ_away = attack_away × defense_home × avg_away_goals.
Lower defense strength = harder to score against (better defense).
